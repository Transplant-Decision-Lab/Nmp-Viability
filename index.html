Evidence & Background

1. Lactate ≤ 2.5 mmol/L at ~4 h

Most consistent viability parameter. Mandatory in the VITTAL trial.

Mergental et al., Nature Communications 2020 (VITTAL)

2. ALT at ~2 h < 6000 U/L

Marker of hepatocellular injury. Used in Cambridge criteria.

Jeddou et al., 2025 review

3. Bile production present

Functional marker. Supporting in VITTAL, core in Groningen protocols.

Mergental et al. (VITTAL)

4. Lactate falling (first 2 h)

Trajectory information. Aligns with Cambridge emphasis on clearance rate.

5. HA flow ≥ 150 & PV flow ≥ 500 mL/min

VITTAL minor criteria for adequate dual perfusion.

Mergental et al. 2020

6. Glucose metabolism

Supporting criterion in VITTAL and Cambridge protocols.

7. Bile pH ≥ 7.45

Cholangiocellular marker (Groningen).

Jeddou et al. review

8. Δ pH (bile − perfusate) ≥ 0.10

More specific biliary epithelial function marker used by the Groningen group.

Jeddou et al. 2025

Relationship to Standard Criteria

  • VITTAL / Birmingham — Core score follows mandatory lactate + supporting items.
  • Cambridge — Shares ALT threshold and focus on lactate dynamics + bile chemistry.
  • Groningen — Bile pH and Δ pH reflect their cholangiocellular criteria.
